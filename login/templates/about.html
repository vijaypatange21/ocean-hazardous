Who We Are

CoastSense is a revolutionary ocean hazard monitoring platform developed by 6 college students from Parul University as part of the Smart India Hackathon. Our innovative solution addresses coastal safety challenges through advanced technology, real-time monitoring, and early warning systems for the Indian National Centre for Ocean Information Services (INCOIS).

Our platform combines cutting-edge satellite technology, artificial intelligence, and community engagement to create a comprehensive coastal safety network. We monitor ocean conditions 24/7, analyze patterns, and provide timely alerts to help communities prepare for and respond to coastal hazards such as tsunamis, storm surges, high waves, and coastal erosion.

Smart India Hackathon Project

CoastSense was developed as part of the Smart India Hackathon, a nationwide initiative to harness the creativity and innovation of students to solve real-world problems. Our team "Dispecables" from Parul University took on the challenge of creating an advanced coastal monitoring system for the Indian National Centre for Ocean Information Services (INCOIS).

The Indian National Centre for Ocean Information Services (INCOIS) is an autonomous organization under the Ministry of Earth Sciences, Government of India. Established in 1999, INCOIS operates the Indian Tsunami Early Warning Centre and provides various ocean-related services including potential fishing zone advisories, ocean state forecasts, and high wave alerts.

Our solution addresses the critical need for real-time coastal hazard monitoring and community engagement, combining modern web technologies with innovative approaches to data visualization and alert systems. CoastSense represents the power of student innovation in solving complex environmental challenges.
