Reporting Guidelines
What to Report:
  • Tsunami: Unusual sea behavior, rapid water level changes
  • Storm Surge: Abnormal water levels during storms
  • High Waves: Exceptionally large waves affecting coastline
  • Swell Surge: Long-period waves from distant storms
  • Coastal Flooding: Water inundation in coastal areas
  • Unusual Tides: Extreme high/low tides beyond normal
Reporting Best Practices:
  • Report incidents as soon as safely possible
  • Include specific time and location details
  • Describe what you observed clearly
  • Include photos/videos if available
  • Mark as urgent only for immediate threats
  • Provide contact information for follow-up
Remember: Your reports help save lives and protect coastal communities. Accurate and timely reporting is crucial for effective hazard management.
